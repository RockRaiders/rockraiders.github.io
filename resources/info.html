Our Team:



RPI Rock Raiders was originally formed to compete the NASA Sample Return Challenge in 2014. This year, because the Sample Return Challenge is closed, the Rock Raiders decided to participate the Mars Rover Challenge.


The Rock Raiders is an interdisciplinary team that brings together talented student engineers and scientists. The team is divided into four technical subteams: Science, Software, Electrical and Mechanical. Each member also signed up to a soft-skill task to help managing the team, such as publicity or budgeting.


The team is extremely collaborative in nature. From weekly general body meetings and subteam meetings to daily open-shop sessions, every student has the opportunity to communicate with the team to present their progress, to receive information on what the team accomplished, and to know what future steps to take to build the mars rover. Additionally, we have integrated online space with Google Hangout, Google Drive, Github, TeamGantt and Slack to share and track information. These collaborative efforts ensure the team is heading to the right direction in completing the robot.
